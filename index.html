Pulsar orbit plotter

This is a little experiment I've done to test JavaScript animations with the D3 library. It shows the orbital movement of a Pulsar and (qualitatively) the change of the observed rotation period and the delay in the arrival times of the pulses due to finite light travel time. Higher-order Relativistic effects are not included. The times at which the Pulsar crosses the ascending and descending nodes are highlighted. The time of mean longitudes 0 and 90 are also plotted, as they are usually referred to in literature. I use the convention used by some timing models (e.g., BT, ELL1) implemented in suites like Tempo2 and PRESTO to assume T0 = TASC for circular orbits. The observer is at infinity, and looks from the bottom of the page.

Insert eccentricity and orientation, then click Refresh page. Then... Start! Don't make it last too long. Memory efficiency is not its thing.

Also, time and orbital dimensions are in arbitrary units for the time being. I will update the code to accommodate for these "uninportant" details later :).
